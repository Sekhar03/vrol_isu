Complete Chargeback Process Flow3.1 High-Level Flow PathFlow: Customer raises dispute â†’ Your Portal â†’ Your Backend â†’ PSP Bank UPI Switch â†’ NPCI UDIR â†’ URCS â†’ Beneficiary Bank â†’ Resolution back through same chainStep-by-Step Transaction Dispute FlowCustomer Reports Issue â€” Customer logs into your portal and reports: transaction debited but not credited, fraud, duplicate charge, goods not delivered, etc.Portal Validates â€” Your portal verifies transaction exists in your DB (match by UPI Txn Ref ID), validates dispute eligibility (within 45-day TAT window), checks if duplicate dispute exists.PSP UPI Switch Sends ReqComplaint â€” Your PSP bank's UPI switch sends the ReqComplaint API to NPCI with: Original Txn ID, dispute type (Complaint/Dispute/Refund), amount, reason code, payer/payee VPA.NPCI UDIR â†’ URCS Lookup â€” NPCI routes the request to URCS. URCS validates: Is transaction settled? Is a dispute already open? Is TAT (45 days) respected? Then creates a complaint/dispute record in URCS.NPCI Notifies Beneficiary Bank â€” NPCI sends RespComplaint-Status Update to the Payee PSP (beneficiary/merchant bank) with complaint details, asking for response.Beneficiary Bank Responds (3 possible outcomes) â€” (a) Confirms credit/delivery â€” chargeback rejected; (b) Agrees to refund â€” initiates ReqComplaint-Refund; (c) Unable to update â€” escalates to formal chargeback after T+3.Chargeback Created in URCS (if unresolved) â€” URCS formally creates a chargeback entry. Remitter bank funds are held. Settlement adjustment is triggered.Auto-Accept/Reject Logic (from Feb 2025) â€” URCS now automatically accepts/rejects the chargeback based on TCC or RET raised by the beneficiary bank in the next settlement cycle. No manual whitelisting needed.Settlement & Credit to Customer â€” If chargeback accepted: NPCI adjusts inter-bank settlement. Remitter bank credits customer account. Your portal gets status update via webhook/API poll.Portal Notifies Customer â€” Your portal updates dispute status, sends notification (SMS/email/push), closes the ticket.3.2 Dispute Categories & When to Use EachUDIR CategorySub-TypeUse CaseWho InitiatesComplaintDRC (Deferred Response Credit)Transaction debited but not credited / pendingPayer PSPComplaintDeemed TransactionNPCI deemed the transaction approved but no creditPayer PSPComplaintP2M â€” Goods Not DeliveredCustomer paid but goods/services not receivedPayer PSPComplaintRefund Not ProcessedMerchant promised refund but not creditedPayer PSPDisputeChargebackFormal dispute after complaint not resolved (T+3 P2P, T+5 P2M)Remitter Bank via URCSDisputeDeferred ChargebackChargeback raised after extended time windowRemitter BankDisputeDRC ConversionDRC complaint converted to dispute if unresolvedURCS auto-triggersRefundPre-Approved RefundMerchant has pre-approved the refund, process via APIPayee PSP / MerchantRefundNon Pre-Approved RefundRefund not pre-approved; goes through NPCI adjudicationPayee PSP3.3 TAT (Turn-Around Time) ReferenceDispute StageTAT / DeadlineNotesCustomer to raise chargebackWithin 45 days from txn dateRevised from July 25, 2024 â€” uniform for all categoriesChargeback can be raised in URCST+0 onwards (same day)Remitter bank can raise from transaction date itselfNPCI maintains transaction data in URCSUp to 45 daysBeyond 45 days, data not searchable unless dispute exists (then 190 days)P2P complaint to chargeback conversionT+1 to T+3If complaint unresolved, URCS converts to chargebackP2M complaint to chargeback conversionT+1 to T+5Longer window for merchant disputesUDIR/ODR complaints should be managedWithin 45 daysBanks must make ODR available to customersReqChkTxn API call limitMax 3 attempts / txn / dayPSP cannot exceed this; audit requiredNPCI compliance penaltiesNo change in processTCC, RET, RRC, DRC penalty processes unchanged4.
